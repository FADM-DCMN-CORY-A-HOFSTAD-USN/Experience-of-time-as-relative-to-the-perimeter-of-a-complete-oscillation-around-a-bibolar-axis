# Provisional Patent Application Draft

**Title:** Relativistic Time-Variance Measurement Apparatus
**Inventor:** Correo Andrew Hofstad

## Abstract
An apparatus and method for measuring and manipulating temporal variance and signal decay within a closed system. The invention utilizes a central bipolar axis and at least two distinct oscillation perimeters. By synchronizing moving particles—such as electrons, photons, or orbital payloads—to execute a complete oscillation at an identical angular velocity across different radial distances, the apparatus physically quantifies the differential in the experience of time. The resulting variance is measured via data corruption, material fatigue, and clock drift.

## Background of the Invention
Conventional measurements of electric coils focus on the exterior environment, such as the energy entering or exiting the coil. The study of what exists within an electric coil and how it compares to the environment outside the same coil is rarely utilized for temporal measurement. There exists a need for a physical apparatus capable of isolating and measuring the decay and variance experienced by objects or signals based on their orbital radius and oscillation perimeter.

## Claims
What is claimed is:

1. An apparatus for measuring temporal variance and signal decay, comprising:
   a central bipolar axis;
   a first signal pathway defining a minimal oscillation perimeter around the bipolar axis;
   a second signal pathway defining a maximum oscillation perimeter around the bipolar axis;
   a synchronization controller configured to propagate a signal through both the first and second pathways at an identical angular velocity; and
   a processor configured to measure the differential state of decay between the signal exiting the first pathway and the signal exiting the second pathway.

2. The apparatus of claim 1, wherein the apparatus is a solid-state logic processor, and the signals propagated through the first and second pathways comprise native-hardware hexadecimal digital code.

3. The apparatus of claim 1, wherein the apparatus is an optoelectronic system, the first and second pathways comprise fiber optic spools, and the signal comprises a split-beam photon stream.

4. An aerospace measurement system comprising:
   a central planetary body acting as a bipolar axis;
   a first satellite positioned at a first radial distance;
   a second satellite positioned at a second, greater radial distance;
   a flight telemetry and propulsion system configured to actively maintain both satellites at an identical angular velocity during oscillation around the central planetary body; and
   a payload within each satellite configured to measure clock drift and component fatigue relative to the differing oscillation perimeters.
