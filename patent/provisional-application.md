# Provisional Patent Application Draft

**Title:** Relativistic Time-Variance Measurement Apparatus
**Inventor:** Correo Andrew Hofstad

## Abstract
An apparatus and method for measuring and manipulating temporal variance and signal decay within a closed system. The invention utilizes a central bipolar axis and at least two distinct oscillation perimeters. By synchronizing moving particles—such as electrons, photons, or orbital payloads—to execute a complete oscillation at an identical angular velocity across different radial distances, the apparatus physically quantifies the differential in the experience of time. The resulting variance is measured via data corruption, material fatigue, and clock drift.

## Background of the Invention
Conventional measurements of electric coils focus on the exterior environment, such as the energy entering or exiting the coil. The study of what exists within an electric coil and how it compares to the environment outside the same coil is rarely utilized for temporal measurement. There exists a need for a physical apparatus capable of isolating and measuring the decay and variance experienced by objects or signals based on their orbital radius and oscillation perimeter.

## Claims
What is claimed is:

1. An apparatus for measuring temporal variance and signal decay, comprising:
   a central bipolar axis;
   a first signal pathway defining a minimal oscillation perimeter around the bipolar axis;
   a second signal pathway defining a maximum oscillation perimeter around the bipolar axis;
   a synchronization controller configured to propagate a signal through both the first and second pathways at an identical angular velocity; and
   a processor configured to measure the differential state of decay between the signal exiting the first pathway and the signal exiting the second pathway.

2. The apparatus of claim 1, wherein the apparatus is a solid-state logic processor, and the signals propagated through the first and second pathways comprise native-hardware hexadecimal digital code.

3. The apparatus of claim 1, wherein the apparatus is an optoelectronic system, the first and second pathways comprise fiber optic spools, and the signal comprises a split-beam photon stream.

4. An aerospace measurement system comprising:
   a central planetary body acting as a bipolar axis;

6. A method for physically measuring temporal variance and signal decay relative to an oscillation perimeter, the method comprising:
   establishing a central bipolar axis within a solid-state native hardware processor;
   routing a first physical pathway for a moving particle at a first radial distance from the bipolar axis;
   routing a second physical pathway for a moving particle at a second, greater radial distance from the bipolar axis;
   injecting a synchronized digital hexadecimal signal into both the first and second pathways simultaneously;
   throttling the execution cycle to an Extremely Low Frequency (ELF) baseline of approximately 7.83 Hz to enforce an identical angular velocity across both pathways; and
   calculating the differential in temporal drift and data corruption between the signal exiting the first pathway and the signal exiting the second pathway.

7. The method of claim 5, wherein calculating the differential comprises measuring the accumulated hardware fatigue and critical decay resulting from the increased load required to maintain the identical angular velocity along the second, greater radial distance.

8. The method of claim 5, wherein calculating the differential comprises comparing the returning hexadecimal string from the second pathway against a baseline payload to detect bit-flip corruption and wave-compression degradation.
   a first satellite positioned at a first radial distance;
   a second satellite positioned at a second, greater radial distance;
   a flight telemetry and propulsion system configured to actively maintain both satellites at an identical angular velocity during oscillation around the central planetary body; and
   a payload within each satellite configured to measure clock drift and component fatigue relative to the differing oscillation perimeters.
   
8. A non-transitory computer-readable storage medium comprising instructions executed by a native hardware processor to diagnose physical structural fatigue within the apparatus of claim 1, the method comprising:
   polling a first hardware register coupled to the exit node of the first signal pathway;
   polling a second hardware register coupled to the exit node of the second signal pathway;
   extracting a first hexadecimal string from the first hardware register and a second hexadecimal string from the second hardware register at a strict execution cycle of approximately 7.83 Hz;
   calculating a physical temporal variance (\(\Delta \tau\)) utilizing the differing radial geometries of the first and second signal pathways; and
   outputting a continuous diagnostic log stream to a command-line interface, wherein the log stream compares the first hexadecimal string to the second hexadecimal string to identify physical bit-state degradation resulting from wave compression limits on the second signal pathway.

9. The non-transitory computer-readable storage medium of claim 8, wherein the diagnostic log strictly records the exact hardware clock cycle at which the increased linear velocity required to maintain the identical angular velocity causes measurable data corruption in the second hardware register.
