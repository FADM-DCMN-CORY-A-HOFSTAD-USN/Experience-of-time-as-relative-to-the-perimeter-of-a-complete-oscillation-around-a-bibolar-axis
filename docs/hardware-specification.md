# Hardware Specification: Relativistic Time-Variance Measurement Apparatus

## 1. System Overview
The following specification details two physical reductions to practice for measuring temporal variance and signal decay relative to the perimeter of an oscillation around a central bipolar axis. Both embodiments force moving particles to share a synchronized temporal execution state while traversing disparate radial perimeters. By comparing the degradation and temporal drift of the resulting signals, the apparatus physically measures the differential in the "experience of time".

---

## 2. Embodiment A: The Micro-Coil Silicon Prototype (Solid-State)

### 2.1 Architecture & Function
This embodiment integrates directly with native hardware processing digital signals in hexadecimal code. The physical apparatus comprises a silicon logic processor equipped with a dual-trace electromagnetic coil array surrounding a central bipolar axis.

* **Alpha Trace (Internal Coil):** Routed at a microscopic radius near the central bipolar axis, traveling along the inside of the electric coil structure. This path defines a minimal oscillation perimeter.
* **Beta Trace (External Coil):** Routed near the outer edge of the silicon die, traveling outside the central coil structure. This path defines a maximum oscillation perimeter.

### 2.2 Operational Mechanics
A synchronized hexadecimal digital signal is injected simultaneously into both the Alpha and Beta traces. The electrical resistance, trace width, and input voltage are physically engineered at the foundry level so that the electrons (the moving particles) in both traces complete their respective oscillations in the exact same measurable execution cycle (sharing an identical angular velocity). 

### 2.3 Measurement Vector
Because the Beta Trace represents a larger orbit, the particles must sustain a higher load to complete the greater perimeter simultaneously. The apparatus features logic gates at the exit nodes of both traces to continuously poll the returning hexadecimal signals. The system calculates temporal variance by measuring:
1.  Accumulated signal decay and data corruption in the hexadecimal string.
2.  Thermal fatigue and critical decay of the surrounding silicon pathways over continuous rapid oscillations.
3.  Micro-fluctuations in clock synchronization at the exit nodes.

## 3. Embodiment B: The Optoelectronic Fiber Ring (Optical)

### 3.1 Architecture & Function
This embodiment utilizes an interference-free photonic environment to isolate temporal variance from electromagnetic noise. The apparatus consists of two precision-machined fiber optic spools wound around a shared physical bipolar axis.

* **Spool Alpha (Short Radius):** A high-density fiber wrap secured tightly against a 1-inch diameter central bipolar axis. 
* **Spool Beta (Long Radius):** A concentric fiber wrap secured at an extended 20-inch radial perimeter around the same axis.

### 3.2 Operational Mechanics
A split-beam laser (operating in a vacuum-like state to minimize standard wave compression) pulses a synchronized photon stream through both spools. Active optoelectronic repeaters and refractive index modulators force the photons in both spools to complete their respective loops simultaneously. 

### 3.3 Measurement Vector
This environment completely isolates the experience of time and physical distance traveled. At the exit nodes, an ultra-high-speed optoelectronic oscilloscope analyzes the output beams. The temporal variance is calculated by measuring:
1.  Phase shift differentials between the Alpha and Beta photon streams.
2.  Wavelength degradation (optical signal decay) resulting from the extended oscillation perimeter of Spool Beta.
3.  Arrival time drift measured at picosecond resolution.
