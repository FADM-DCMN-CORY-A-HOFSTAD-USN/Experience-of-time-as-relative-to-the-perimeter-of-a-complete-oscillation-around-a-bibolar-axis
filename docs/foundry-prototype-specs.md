## 1. Trace Geometry and Layout
The silicon die must accommodate a dual-trace electromagnetic coil array surrounding a central bipolar logic axis.
* **Trace Alpha (Internal Coil):** Must be routed at the minimum safe microscopic radius from the central axis. 
* **Trace Beta (External Coil):** Must be routed along the maximum outer perimeter of the silicon die.

## 2. Electrical Constraints & Angular Velocity Synchronization
To properly evaluate signal decay relative to oscillation perimeter, Trace Alpha and Trace Beta must share a synchronized temporal execution cycle.
* The foundry must engineer the resistance (ohms) and width of Trace Beta to be physically distinct from Trace Alpha. 
* A hexadecimal signal injected simultaneously into both nodes must arrive at the exit logic gates at the exact same clock cycle. 
* *Engineering Note:* Because Trace Beta travels a larger oscillation perimeter, the moving particles (electrons) must sustain a higher load to achieve this synchronization.

## 3. Thermal and Load Tolerances
Objects when put under great volumes of pressure instantly decay, break apart, and crumble. The decay of metal is visible almost instantly when placed under a load which creates pressure that instantly ages the metal. 
* The foundry must utilize high-durability substrate shielding around Trace Beta.
* Trace Beta will be subjected to critical, rapid-oscillation loads designed to intentionally induce structural fatigue. The thermal envelope must prevent cascading failure to the central logic axis during prolonged testing.
